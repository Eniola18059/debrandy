The founder

"Good solar is not about quantity. It is about engineering."

Jeremiah founded Debrandy Solar Solutions on a belief that's simple to say and easy to skip in practice: a solar system should be built around what you actually need to power, not around how many panels look impressive on a roof.

That belief came from watching the industry get it backwards too often — installers stacking panels without checking if the battery could store what they generate, or selling inverters that buckle the moment a fridge and AC run at once. The result is systems that look complete on day one and fail within months.

"A well-designed 6kVA system can outperform a poorly planned 10kVA setup when the architecture is right."

Every battery delivered to a client is inspected before it leaves Alaba — checked for performance and safety, not just packaged and sent. That's the standard the business runs on: not just selling equipment, but delivering power people can actually trust.

Our story

From one honest sizing job to a name Alaba trusts.

Debrandy Solar Solutions didn't start with a showroom or a big launch — it started with Jeremiah fixing what other installers kept getting wrong: systems that looked fine on paper but failed the first time a fridge and AC ran together.

Word spread the way it does at Alaba — one client telling another that the sizing was honest, the batteries were inspected, and the system actually held up. That's still how most new clients find us today.

Since then, the work has grown from single power tanks for apartments to full hybrid installations for shops, hotels, and growing businesses across Lagos — with the same load-first approach on every job, no matter the size.
